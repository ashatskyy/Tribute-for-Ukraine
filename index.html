Motherland We Are Proud With

Here's the main historical facts about Ukraine:

  • 10th c. - The first known archaeological and historical evidence of the modern "Trident" symbol adopted as the coat of arms of the modern Ukraine
  • 10-11th cc. - Kievan Rus' largest and most powerful state in Europe
  • mid 13th c. - The Mongol invasion devastated Kievan Rus'
  • from the 13th c. - The period of division of the territories of modern Ukraine between appanage principalitys, neighboring European states, and the successor of the Golden Horde — the Muscovite Empire
  • 1492 - The first historical mention of Cossacks, the military and political organization of which — Zaporozhian Sich served as a democratic prototype of the modern Ukrainian state
  • 1710 - Constitution of Pylyp Orlyk, one of the first European democratic Constitutions established a Cossack parliament
  • 1775 - The abolition of the Zaporizhian Sich by the Russian (Muscovite) Empire
  • 1791-1918 - The rise of Ukrainian national movement and attempts to form an independent Ukrainian state
  • 1918 - Proclaimed on part of the territory of the former Russian Empire, the Ukrainian People's Republic
  • 1919 - Unification of the Ukrainian People's Republic and the West Ukrainian People's Republic
  • 1922 - Formation of the Ukrainian Socialist Radyanska Republic as a result of the seizure of Ukraine by Bolshevik Russia
  • 1941-1945 - 7 million Ukrainians fight in the ranks of the Red Army against fascism
  • 1954 - The final formation of the borders of the modern Ukrainian state as a result of the transfer of Crimea to the jurisdiction of the Ukrainian Soviet Socialist Republic
  • 1991 - Independence of Ukraine approved by 92% of the Ukrainian in a referendum
  • 1994 - Budapest Memorandum. Ukraine's renunciation of nuclear weapons in exchange for guarantees of security and inviolability of borders. The memorandum was signed by: the leaders of Ukraine, Russia, Great Britain and the United States. Before renunciation of nuclear status, Ukraine possessed the second largest arsenal of nuclear weapons in the world
  • 1995 - Adoption of the Constitution of Ukraine, Article 10, which in particular guarantees the free use and development of the russian language
  • 1999 - Complete elimination of bandit groups in Ukraine and establishment of a state of law at all levels of the social system
  • 2003 - Ukrainian independence and state borders have already been recognized by 140 countries
  • 2004 - The Orange Revolution is a peaceful Ukrainian people's struggle against the attempts of oligarchic groups supported by Russia to come to power
  • 2008 - Ukraine — a peaceful, multinational, developing country. The new generation of Ukrainians is laying the foundation for future economic and cultural prosperity. The absolute majority of Ukrainians want to see their country uncorrupted and part of the European Union
  • 2010 - As a result of the consequences of the global crisis, with the secret support of Putin's Russian dictatorship, on a wave of populist promises of modernization and a European future for Ukraine, representatives of Eastern Ukrainian corrupt post-gangster groups, led by the newly elected President Yanukovych, come to power
  • 2010-2013 - Yanukovych's rule is turning into repressions of all social levels unprecedented in the history of the Ukrainian state. The country is sliding into the dark past of the 20th century
  • 2014 - Euromaidan. The refusal to sign the European Association Agreement by the Yanukovych government becomes the last straw that broke the camel's back for the Ukrainian people. A people revolution takes place in the country. The Yanukovych regime is overthrown. The new government is elected by popular democratic vote
  • 2014 - Having lost his representative in Ukraine and seeing in the democratic revolution of Ukraine a direct threat to his power, the Russian dictator Putin begins a war against Ukraine. The Crimean peninsula and parts of the Donetsk and Luhansk regions are being seized. Patriotic volunteer military formations of Ukrainians put up fierce resistance to the enemy.
  • 2014 - The 5-th Democratic elections of the President of Ukraine
  • 2019 - The 6-th Democratic elections of the President of Ukraine
  • 2014-2021 - Feeling impunity due to lack of decisive action from the guarantor countries of the Budapest Memorandum the dictatorial regime of Russia is increasing its efforts to foment war in Ukraine. It is actively distributes fakes about interethnic conflict in the country
  • 2021 - The 30th anniversary of Ukraine's independence. Despite the ongoing war with Russia in the East of the country, which undermines the state's strength, Ukraine is building its military forces. From a former corrupt and ineffective structure, the Ukrainian army is being transformed into a powerful, highly motivated defense organization created according to NATO standards
  • 2022 - Having discarded all previous claims about the alleged interethnic civil conflict in Ukraine and Russia's non-intervention, Putin's dictatorial regime begins an open, large-scale invasion of the country
  • 2022-2024 - The heroic resistance of the Ukrainian army and people will destroy plans for the lightning seizure of Ukraine by Russia. The open phase of war that continues to this day has led to a huge number of victims among the civilian population and hundreds of thousands of war crimes committed by russian military
  • 2024 - Ukraine, not receiving sufficient assistance from its Western allies, is developing its military infrastructure and becoming a world leader in the production of some types of weapons. 90% of the country's population supports the waging a war of liberation until the complete expulsion of the aggressor from the territory of the Ukraine
  • "Right now and right in Ukraine the fate of the world order based on rules, humanity, and predictability is being decided."
    — President of Ukraine V. Zelensky
